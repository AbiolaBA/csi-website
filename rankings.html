Reading the numbers

The CSI is standardised within each year: a score of +1 means one standard deviation more consumption sovereignty than the world in that year; −1 means one below.

The African mean is −0.82 against +0.71 for Europe & Central Asia — a persistent gap of over 1.5 standard deviations. Yet the continent contains striking variation: South Africa (+1.14) ranks in the global top 25, with Tunisia, Morocco, Kenya and Mauritius all near or above the world mean, while conflict-affected economies dominate the global bottom.

That within-Africa variation is exactly what makes the index usable for policy targeting — it identifies which economies convert capability into sovereign consumption, and which import what they could make.
