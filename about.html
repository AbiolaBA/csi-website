The lab

About the Consumer Intelligence Lab

A research programme on the consumption side of development — built on the conviction that what a nation consumes is a structural lever of its industrialisation, not a footnote to what it produces.

Mission

Development economics has read national capability from the production side for two decades — brilliantly, through the economic-complexity programme. The Consumer Intelligence Lab builds the missing half: rigorous, reproducible measurement of how nations consume, and of the gap between what economies could make and what their consumers buy.

The stakes are highest for the Global South. When consumption leans on imports of goods a country could produce, income that would fund domestic jobs funds foreign producers instead — the consumption multiplier leaks abroad. Measuring that leak precisely, product by product and year by year, turns an old and often ideological debate into a targeting problem industrial policy can actually solve.

Reject Say's Law; assert consumer sovereignty. Consumption is a decision — and decisions can be well or poorly aligned with a nation's long-run interest.
The missing half of the story Whether a country turns its capability into goods that supply its own consumers — that is what the lab measures.

How we work

Measurement first. Constructs earn their names. When verification showed our v0.1 index was inverted, we published the failure and rebuilt the method — the account is kept in the methodology deliberately.

Reproducibility as a hard constraint. Every published number traces to public data, a recorded data vintage, and an open pipeline that rebuilds it with one command.

Our own harshest reviewer. The lab publishes its critical reviews alongside its results — including the finding that our current tiers measure different constructs than the founding thesis targeted, and precisely what it would take to close that gap.

Diagnosis, not protectionism. The CSI measures a gap. Nothing in the lab's work advocates tariffs; the gap closes through quality, capability and value systems — with the consumer sovereign throughout.

Produced by Ingenious Research Laboratory

The Consumer Intelligence Lab is a research initiative produced by Ingenious Research Laboratory. Ingenious Research Laboratory is the parent organisation under which the lab's indexes, datasets and reproducible analysis are developed and published — providing the research infrastructure, standards and stewardship behind the Consumption Sovereignty Index and the wider index family.

Consumer Intelligence Lab is the flagship research programme; Ingenious Research Laboratory is the organisation that produces it. New indexes beyond the CSI family will be developed and released under the same umbrella.

Roadmap

  1. Peer-adjusted CSI — isolating the value-system signal from the development effect (in progress).
  2. Category-level preference measurement — the status-premium index concentrated where the xenocentrism signal actually lives.
  3. Income-leakage index — cents per consumption dollar funding domestic labour and value added.
  4. The institutional layer — consumption sovereignty, institutions and colonial legacy (WGI, COLDAT), in the Acemoglu–Robinson tradition.
  5. New indexes beyond the CSI family — the lab's architecture (open pipeline, versioned methodology, published critique) is built to host them.
